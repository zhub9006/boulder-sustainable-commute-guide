# 🌿 Sustainable Travel Tips for Boulder Commuters

## Why Choose Green Commuting?

### Environmental Impact
- A typical car commute of 4 km produces **~0.8 kg of CO₂** per trip
- Annualizing that: **~200 kg of CO₂ per year** for a 5-day work week
- If all Boulder residents switched to active transport for short trips, we could reduce community emissions by **~15%**

### Personal Benefits
- **Health:** Active commuting reduces risk of heart disease, diabetes, and obesity
- **Mental Health:** Walking and biking reduce stress, anxiety, and improve mood
- **Productivity:** Commuters who exercise before work report higher focus and energy
- **Financial:** Save **~$3,000/year** by reducing car use (fuel + maintenance + parking)

## Tips for Getting Started

### 🚶 Walking
1. **Start small:** Walk one day a week, then increase
2. **Gear up:** Invest in good walking shoes and a quality backpack
3. **Stay hydrated:** At 5,430 ft elevation, you lose moisture faster
4. **Buddy system:** Find a walking partner for accountability
5. **Track it:** Use a pedometer or app to see your progress

### 🚴 Biking
1. **Test the route:** Do a weekend test ride before your first work commute
2. **Pack light:** Use panniers or a backpack — avoid heavy loads
3. **Layer up:** Boulder's temperature can swing 20°F+ between morning and afternoon
4. **Secure your bike:** 1800 Broadway has covered bike parking — use it!
5. **Maintain your bike:** Regular tune-ups keep you safe and efficient

### 🚌 Combined Options
- **Bike + Bus:** Fold your bike and take RTD for part of the route
- **Park and Walk:** Park at a transit center and walk the last mile
- **Carpool + Walk:** Carpool partway, then walk the remainder

## Seasonal Guide

### 🌸 Spring (March–May)
- Ideal walking and biking weather
- Start early to avoid afternoon wind
- Wildflowers along 19th Street

### ☀️ Summer (June–August)
- Ride early (7–9 AM) or late (5–7 PM) to avoid heat
- Carry extra water — altitude + heat = dehydration risk
- Light-colored clothing reflects sun better

### 🍂 Fall (September–November)
- Beautiful foliage on the route
- Temps cool down — perfect for biking
- Shorter days — bring a headlamp for evening commutes

### ❄️ Winter (December–February)
- Walking: Use traction devices on icy sidewalks
- Biking: Snow tires and layered clothing are essential
- Both: Allow extra time and stay visible with lights/reflectors
