## 🚶 Walking Commute

- **Estimated Travel Time:** ~48 minutes
- **Distance:** 4.03 km
- **Calories Burned:** ~300–350 kcal
- **CO₂ Saved:** ~0.8 kg per trip (vs. driving)

### Route Summary
1. **Oak Avenue** — Head south from home on Oak Avenue
2. **19th Street** — Continue south on 19th Street (longest segment)
3. **20th St. / 20th Street** — Navigate through the 20th Street corridor
4. **Pearl Street** — Cross Pearl Street, a major Boulder artery
5. **18th Street** — Continue south on 18th Street
6. **Canyon Boulevard** — Arrive at your destination near Canyon Blvd

> 💡 **Walking Tip:** Boulder's flat terrain in this corridor makes for a pleasant walk. Consider listening to a podcast or enjoying the mountain views along the way!

## 🚴 Biking Commute

- **Estimated Travel Time:** ~16 minutes
- **Distance:** 4.03 km
- **Calories Burned:** ~150–200 kcal
- **CO₂ Saved:** ~0.8 kg per trip (vs. driving)

### Route Summary
The biking route follows the same corridor as walking, but at a faster pace:
1. **Oak Avenue** — Depart from Oak Avenue
2. **19th Street** — Main stretch southbound
3. **20th Street corridor** — Through the 20th Street area
4. **Pearl Street** — Use designated bike lanes where available
5. **18th Street** — Continue south
6. **Canyon Boulevard** — Arrive at 1800 Broadway

> 💡 **Biking Tip:** Boulder has excellent bike infrastructure. Use the designated bike lanes on 19th and 20th Streets for a safer commute. A bike commuter saves ~200 kg of CO₂ per month compared to driving!

## 🌎 Why Green Commuting Matters

- **Climate:** Transportation is the largest source of greenhouse gas emissions in the US. Every car trip you replace helps.
- **Health:** Walking and biking commute basics improve cardiovascular health, reduce stress, and boost productivity.
- **Community:** Fewer cars means safer streets, less noise, and more vibrant neighborhoods.
- **Savings:** The average Boulder commuter saves ~$3,000/year by reducing car use.

## 📝 Tips for Boulder Commuters

### Walking
- Wear comfortable, weather-appropriate shoes
- Carry a reusable water bottle — Boulder's altitude increases dehydration
- Use the paved Path along Boulder Creek for a scenic alternative

### Biking
- Use bike lanes on 19th and 20th Streets
- Consider a bike rack for your office — 1800 Broadway has covered bike parking
- Winter commuting? Boulder's bike infrastructure is plowed, but dress in layers
